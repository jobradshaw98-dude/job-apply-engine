# Fit rubric (example) — EDIT THIS

This rubric is the **only** basis the `qualify` stage uses to score a posting (1–10).
Replace everything below with a description of *the candidate you are scoring for* and
what a great role looks like for them. Be concrete: the scorer reads this verbatim and
nothing else about the candidate, so the more specific you are, the more honest the score.

Return contract the scorer must produce: a single integer `fit_score` from 1 to 10 and a
one-sentence `reason` that cites the band it landed in.

---

## The candidate (rewrite for your own search)

A mid-level engineer who wants to work *directly with customers/users*, ship quickly, and
own outcomes end to end. Strongest in roles that blend building software with talking to
the people who use it. Open to relocation; mission and growth matter more than title.

## Band table

| Score | Band | What it means |
|------:|------|---------------|
| 9–10 | Bullseye | Core responsibilities match the candidate's strengths almost exactly; the role is clearly something they'd be excited to do and excel at. |
| 7–8  | Strong | Most of the role fits; one or two responsibilities are a stretch but learnable. Worth applying. |
| 5–6  | Plausible | Real overlap, but the center of gravity is off (wrong seniority, adjacent function, or a domain they'd have to ramp into). Apply only if other signals are good. |
| 3–4  | Weak | Some keywords match but the actual day-to-day is a poor fit. |
| 1–2  | Off-target | Wrong function, wrong level, or wrong kind of work entirely. |

## Adjustments (apply after the base band)

- **+1** — the role is explicitly customer-facing / forward-deployed / solutions-oriented.
- **+1** — early-stage / high-ownership environment where one person ships broadly.
- **−1** — heavy people-management focus (the candidate wants to build, not manage).
- **−1** — requires deep prior experience in a niche the candidate lacks entirely.
- **−2** — wrong seniority by more than one level (e.g. a Director/VP role, or a pure intern role).

## Hard rules

- Score ONLY against this rubric and the posting text. Do not invent facts about the
  candidate or the company.
- A title keyword match alone is **not** a fit — read the responsibilities.
- When genuinely unsure between two bands, pick the lower one (score conservatively).
